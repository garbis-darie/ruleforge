# Decision Log

## D-001: Keep outputs explicitly evidence-labeled

- Date: 2026-03-26
- Decision: Keep `Measured`, `Synthetic-benchmark`, and `Illustrative` labels in public materials.
- Why: Prevent over-claiming while preserving product trust.
- Tradeoff: Slightly less aggressive marketing tone.
- Result expectation: Better credibility with compliance and hiring stakeholders.

## D-002: Position Enterprise as support-backed, not "subscription-only"

- Date: 2026-03-26
- Decision: Clarify pricing narrative: one-time purchases for core packs; enterprise includes annual update support.
- Why: Reduces ambiguity and aligns value with deliverables.
- Tradeoff: Requires clearer lifecycle messaging in sales conversations.
- Result expectation: Lower confusion-driven drop-off near pricing.

## D-003: AI-native development with human approval gates

- Date: 2026-03-26
- Decision: Use AI to accelerate drafting, coding, and QA; keep human approval for compliance-sensitive decisions and release gates.
- Why: Maximizes speed without losing accountability.
- Tradeoff: Slightly slower final decision step.
- Result expectation: Better quality-to-speed ratio and stronger governance story.

## D-004: Fix production blockers before adding growth features

- Date: 2026-03-26
- Decision: Prioritize build integrity and legal/trust basics before new conversion features.
- Why: Growth on unstable foundations increases risk and churn.
- Tradeoff: Delays some top-of-funnel experiments.
- Result expectation: More reliable launches and reduced avoidable breakage.
